What is Al-Madd Al-Tabi'i?
Al-Madd Al-Tabi'i is the natural elongation — the foundation of all Madd rules. It occurs when a letter of Madd follows a vowel without any hamzah or sukoon.
بِسْمِ ٱللَّهِ ٱلرَّحْمَٰنِ ٱلرَّحِيمِ
Notice the natural elongation on "نِ" and "يمِ" — this is Al-Madd Al-Tabi'i in action.
Duration: exactly 2 harakat. Cannot be increased or decreased — hence "natural" (Tabi'i). This rule applies when one of the three Madd letters (Alif, Waw, Ya) appears without a hamzah or sukoon following it.
Key Takeaways
Al-Madd Al-Tabi'i is always 2 harakat — neither more nor less. Recognise the three Madd letters and practice reciting verses that contain them.
